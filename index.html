Understanding Immune Surveillance in Cancer

Developing next-generation immunotherapies through mechanistic insights into DNA damage responses and tumor immunity

Xiaoxiao Ma, Ph.D. | Assistant Professor

Explore Research Join Our Lab

Welcome to the Ma Lab

Our laboratory investigates the fundamental principles that determine the strength and consequence of immune surveillance during tumorigenesis and therapy. We focus on understanding how mutagenesis processes and DNA damage responses impact immune recognition and escape, particularly in hypermutated and DDR-deficient tumors.

Our research aims to leverage mechanistic insights to develop next-generation immunotherapies for more efficient cancer prevention and treatment. We combine computational approaches with experimental validation to uncover new therapeutic targets and biomarkers.
