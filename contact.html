Research Opportunities

Graduate Student Rotations

We welcome rotation students from various UAB graduate programs including Genetics, Genomics & Bioinformatics (GGB), Cancer Biology, Immunology, and related fields.

Available Rotation Projects (2025)

Project 1: EGFR T790M Neoantigen Discovery and TCR Engineering

Duration: 8-10 weeks
Skills Gained: Computational biology, neoantigen prediction, TCR analysis, immunogenomics
Description: Identify and validate neoantigens arising from EGFR T790M resistance mutations using our established TCR discovery pipeline. Students will learn computational approaches for neoantigen prediction and experimental validation techniques.

Ideal for students with: Interest in computational biology, basic programming skills (R/Python preferred), immunology background helpful but not required
Project 2: DNA Polymerase Mutations and Immune Landscape Analysis

Duration: 10-12 weeks
Skills Gained: Bioinformatics, tumor immunology, multi-omics data analysis, biomarker discovery
Description: Characterize immune microenvironments in POLE/POLD1 mutated tumors and develop predictive models for checkpoint blockade responses. This project extends our Nature Genetics publication.

Ideal for students with: Strong analytical skills, interest in cancer genomics, experience with statistical analysis
Project 3: Hypoalbuminemia Impact on Immunotherapy Efficacy

Duration: 8-10 weeks
Skills Gained: Clinical data analysis, biomarker discovery, statistical modeling, survival analysis
Description: Analyze large patient cohorts to understand how low serum albumin affects immune checkpoint inhibitor efficacy and identify potential intervention strategies.

Ideal for students with: Interest in translational research, clinical data analysis, basic statistics knowledge

How to Apply for Rotations

  1. Email Dr. Ma with your CV and a brief statement of interest
  2. Include which project(s) interest you most and why
  3. Mention your current program and expected rotation timeline
  4. We'll schedule a meeting to discuss projects and expectations

Postdoctoral Fellowship Positions

We are actively seeking motivated postdoctoral fellows to join our team and lead independent research projects in cancer immunotherapy.

Ideal Candidates

  • Education: Ph.D. in immunology, cancer biology, computational biology, genetics, or related field
  • Experience: Background in cancer research, immunotherapy, genomics, or bioinformatics
  • Skills: Strong analytical and communication skills, publication record, ability to work independently
  • Interests: Passion for translational research with clinical applications
  • Mentorship: Willingness to mentor graduate students and contribute to lab culture

Research Opportunities

  • Lead neoantigen discovery and TCR engineering projects
  • Develop novel immunotherapy combination strategies
  • Analyze large-scale genomic and immunologic datasets
  • Collaborate with clinical teams on translational studies
  • Pursue independent funding opportunities (K99/R00, individual fellowships)

Application Requirements

Please send the following materials to xma2@uab.edu:

  • Current CV including publications and conference presentations
  • Cover letter describing research interests and career goals (1-2 pages)
  • Contact information for three professional references
  • Representative publication (PDF) or thesis chapter
