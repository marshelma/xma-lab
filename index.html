Understanding Immune Surveillance in Cancer

Developing next-generation immunotherapies through mechanistic insights into DNA damage responses and tumor immunity

Xiaoxiao Ma, Ph.D. | Assistant Professor

Department of Genetics, School of Medicine

University of Alabama at Birmingham

Research Focus

DNA Damage & Immune Recognition

Investigating how mutagenesis processes and DNA damage responses impact immune recognition and escape in hypermutated and DDR-deficient tumors.

Immunotherapy Resistance

Developing novel approaches to target drug-resistant mutations and understand mechanisms of checkpoint blockade resistance.

Neoantigen Discovery

Establishing experimental and computational pipelines for neoantigen TCR discovery and immunogenicity prediction.

POLE/POLD1 Mutations

Characterizing functional landscapes of DNA polymerase mutations in checkpoint blockade-dependent anti-tumor immunity.

Background

Dr. Ma's research focuses on understanding the fundamental principles that determine the strength and consequence of immune surveillance during tumorigenesis and therapy. She completed her Ph.D. at City of Hope National Medical Center and postdoctoral training at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center and Cleveland Clinic.
